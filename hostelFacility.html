Campus Life



Hostels and Accomodation

Mother Teresa Women's University offers comfortable and affordable hostel accommodations to cater to the diverse student population, many of whom come from various regions. The university currently maintains several hostels, namely Mullai Block, Thamarai Block, Ali Block, and Ananthagiri Hostel. These accommodations play a crucial role in providing a conducive living environment for students pursuing different programs. Each hostel is strategically located within the university campus, ensuring easy access to academic facilities. The university's commitment to providing inclusive housing is reflected in the diverse options available. Among these hostels, Mullai, Thamarai, and Ali Blocks cater to specific needs, while Ananthagiri Hostel serves as a dedicated accommodation facility. These hostels collectively accommodate a substantial number of students, fostering a sense of community and support among the residents. The university continually strives to enhance the living experience, offering amenities such as well-equipped rooms, recreational spaces, and connectivity options. In alignment with the university's ethos, the hostels are equipped with essential facilities to ensure the well-being and convenience of the students. These include amenities like fans, adequate lighting, water facilities, recreational centers, playing grounds, newspapers, and magazines. Furthermore, each hostel features television with cable connectivity, Wi-Fi accessibility, and a hygienic cooking facility. The provision of 24-hour security, hot water facilities, and dedicated committees for grievance redressal and procurement underscore the institution's commitment to maintaining a conducive living environment. Additionally, telephone facilities are available for students to stay connected with the outside world. Overall, the hostels at Mother Teresa Women's University are designed to create a holistic and enriching residential experience for its diverse student community.

The efficient functioning and administration of the hostels at Mother Teresa Women's University are meticulously managed by a dedicated team comprising various roles and responsibilities. The administrative structure includes key positions such as (a) the Warden/Registrar, who plays a pivotal role in overseeing overall hostel operations, (b) Hostel Administrator, responsible for the day-to-day management, (c) Deputy Wardens contributing to the smooth functioning of specific aspects, (d) Residential Supervisors ensuring a secure and comfortable living environment, (e) Supervisors handling specific tasks related to hostel management, (f) Assistant Section Officers involved in administrative support, and (g) Office Assistants and other staff members collectively contributing to the well-being of the residents.

The administrations of all hostels are under the control of CENTRAL OFFICE FOR HOSTEL ADMINISTRATION
