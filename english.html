About

The Department amalgamates disciplines in dynamic dialogue with each other, ensuring both specific knowledge and a more general ability to think. The Traditional courses will be studied in tandem with cutting edge courses on "Digital Humanities" for a wide exposure to different genres. It emphasizes research on Global Feminist literatures , Eco Studies, Diaspora studies and ELT. This wide range of courses offered in M.A., M.Phil.,and Ph.D., will prepare the graduates for a variety of careers in higher education, and at any field that requires an agile mind able to traverse across borders. It imparts efficient Communicative Skills to the students to be competent, through its well equipped Foreign Language Lab and also offers Certificate Courses in French and German. The Department of English & Foreign Languages established in 1985 has undertaken resarch projects and is in charge of Publication Division of the University. It publishes the University's official Newsletter the "News Flash" and a journal "Gender & Progress". The Department has a Foreign Language Lab which caters to the needs of rural students through imparting Communication Skills Spoken English and Advaned Grammer Classes are conducted. The Department offers courses with a main focus on Feminist Literature and Women's Writings.

Mission
  • The mission of the English Department is to foster a deep appreciation for literature, language, and critical thinking among students.
  • We aim to provide a dynamic learning environment that encourages creativity, analytical skills, and effective communication.
  • Our mission includes cultivating a love for diverse literary traditions, promoting research in language studies, and nurturing a community of lifelong learners.
  • We are committed to empowering students with the skills to express themselves eloquently, think critically, and engage meaningfully with the world through language and literature.
    • Vision
  • The English Department envisions becoming a center of excellence in literary and linguistic studies, recognized for its academic rigor and innovative teaching approaches.
  • We aspire to produce graduates who are not only proficient in language arts but also possess a deep understanding of the cultural, historical, and global contexts of literature.
  • Our vision includes fostering a supportive and inclusive academic community that celebrates diversity of thought, expression, and literary exploration.
  • We aim to contribute to the advancement of knowledge in language and literature through impactful research, publications, and community engagement.
